Help

This page is meant to provide you with help on how to use dcyphr.


Read or find an article

Use the search bar in the navigation panel or on the homepage to search our database of summaries. Or go to browse to peruse what we have. If you don't see the article, that means it hasn't been summarized yet.

Request an article

If the article you want summarized is not available yet, go to the requests tab and input the relevant information about the article for it to be summarized.

Summarize or edit an article

If you have read an article and wish to contribute a summary, just log in to your account and go to the contribute tab and click on the relevant task. If it hasn't been requested, you can request it first under requests and then accept the task on the contribute page.

If you notice an inaccuracy or inconsistency in one of the summaries that you are reading, just click the edit button to fix the mistake and refine the summary. You can also earn points through summarizing and editing!

Other reasons to contribute and edit summaries are to test yourself to see how well you know the information because if you can't explain it simply, then you don't know it well enough! Also, your contributions will benefit future readers and have a large impact on scientific accessibility.

Navigating the site

Navigation bar at the top of each page will take you to the main pages. The dcyphr logo will take you back to the home page. Once you login, you will have access to the profile page and contribute page. The homepage and navigation bar have a search tool. The homepage also has the leaderboard and mission statement. Enjoy dcyphr!

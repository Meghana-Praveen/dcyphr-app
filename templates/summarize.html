Introduction:

  • The introduction gives extensive background on the gene or protein of interest
  • Report why the authors chose the particular subject (e.g. gene of interest) to study
  • Spend 1-3 sentences reporting a few pieces of background information directly relating to the experiment or study
  • Report any unclear terms or concepts that the authors define


Aims:

  • Find the purpose of the research and report this

Methods:

  • Only report important techniques
  • When reporting a technique, name the technique itself and state the purpose of its use
  • There should be causal or chronological order to this section

Results:

  • Report the corresponding results of each method that you reported
  • Summarize in a couple sentences figures that directly relate to the results that you report. Report figures and corresponding results together.

Conclusion:

  • Report the meaning and significance of each result you summarized
  • Report any conclusions the authors make from their results

  • Use active voice
  • Minimize nominalization
  • Keep subjects near verbs near objects
  • Use simple word substitutions (under 3 syllables)
  • Restrict sentences to one idea per sentence
  • Make dependent clauses in the middle of sentence a separate sentence
    • Ex. The ball, which was blue, rolled down the hill. → The ball was blue. It rolled down the hill. → The blue ball rolled down the hill.
  • Qualify all the pronouns you use
  • Introduce paragraphs with main idea sentence
  • State only the ideas of the original text
